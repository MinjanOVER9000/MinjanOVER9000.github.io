The History and the Orign of the legendary game......
Chess

The most commonly held belief is that chess originated in India, where it was called Chaturanga, which appears to have been invented in the 6th century AD.
Although this is commonly believed, it is thought that Persians created a more modern version of the game after the Indians.

Objectives
of Chess

The objective of the game chess is to capture as many of your opponents pieces and place the king under a checkmate, which is a state for the opponent where they cant prevent their king from being captured.

Pieces

The King.

The Material chess

You might be like, OK. I got this, so I have to go and checkmate the other king. Well, the problem is that you need to get past 15 different pieces. The solution is material chess. This is when one plays and trys to get chess pieces off the opponent so the enemy is easier to checkmate. The queen is worth 9 points, rook 5, knight and bishop 3, and the pawns are worth one. The King is worth the game.

How to move in Chess

So in order for the game of chess to progress, the player has to move their pieces.
Here I will be telling you how to move each piece in the game. and a good thing to remember. White always moves first.

  • The King

    The King is the most valuable piece in the game, and also one of the most limited. It can only move one space in any direction for one turn.

  • The Queen

    The Queen is the most powerful piece in the game. If you lose your queen, then one would probably lose the game. The queen can move in any direction infinitly. Cannot jump over pieces.

  • The Rook

    The Rook is a important piece in the game that is usually brought out in the end game. The Rook can move infinitly in and vertical or horizontal line that it is in. Also cannot jump over pieces

  • The Knight

    The knight is a rather special pieces and is used in a very tactical way. This is because the knight can jump over enemy pieces and allie pieces. The knight moves in a "L" shaped position

  • The Bishop

    The Bishop is somewhat like the rook but instead of moving horizonatally and vertically, it can only move diagonnaly, it is also a tactical piece in the game.

  • The Pawn

    The pawn is a weak piece on the board, however, to compensate for the weakness of the pawn, they decided to add the concept of promotion, which is if a pawn reaches the end of the board, than the pawn can turn into another piece of choice. The pawn can move one space forward and two if the pawn has not moved yet. To capture another piece, a pawn has to be one square diagonal from the other piece
